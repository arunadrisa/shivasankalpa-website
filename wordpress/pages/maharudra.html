About the Puraścaraṇa

Recognizing the immeasurable value of the Vedas, it is our sacred responsibility to contribute towards their preservation, nourishment, and propagation.

With the boundless grace and divine blessings of His Holiness Jagadguru Sri Sri Bharati Tirtha Mahasannidhanam and His Holiness Jagadguru Sri Sri Vidhushekhara Bharati Sannidhanam of Dakshinamnaya Sri Sharada Peetham, Sringeri, and under the auspices of Sri Shivasankalpa Vṛnda, this Mahā Rudra Puraścaraṇa is organized for:

  • Loka Kalyāṇa — universal welfare and the well-being of all
  • The support and development of Veda Gurukulas — strengthening the institutions that preserve the Vedas

We humbly invite all devotees and well-wishers to participate and contribute through tanu, mana, and dhana — offering their service, devotion, and resources.

Spiritual Significance of the Rudrādhyāya

रुदं द्रावयति नाशयति इति रुद्रः

"Rudra is the compassionate Lord who melts away the devotee's sorrow and destroys all suffering."

As described in the Shiva Purana, the ultimate goal of human birth is liberation from the cycle of birth and death and the attainment of Kaivalya — Supreme Liberation.

In Kali Yuga, Bhagavan has bestowed two primary paths: Karma Mārga (the Path of Action) and Jñāna Mārga (the Path of Knowledge). Selfless actions performed with devotion lead one towards knowledge and liberation.

The Yajur Veda, particularly the Rudrādhyāya, prescribes sacred chanting and homa that remove accumulated karmas, bestow inner purification, and lead the seeker towards Amṛtatva — immortality.

पातकानि विनश्यन्ति यावन्ति रुद्रजपतः |
भुवि तावन्ति पापानि जन्यन्ते न नरैर्मुने ||
शिवनामनि तरे प्राप्ते संसाराब्धिं तरन्ति ते |
संसारमूलपापानि तानि नश्यन्त्यसंशयः ||

— Shiva Purana
